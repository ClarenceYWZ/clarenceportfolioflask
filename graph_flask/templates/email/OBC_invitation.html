Outcome Based Contract Background

As demand for security services continue to rise, increasing manpower to meet service demand is not sustainable given the security industry's manpower constraints. Therefore, the Security Industry Transformation Map (ITM) recommends four key strategies to enable both security service buyers and providers to move away from a headcount-based model. This is done by shifting the security industry's reliance on manpower to delivering integrated security solutions that integrate technology, manpower and processes. One of the strategies is to drive change in the procurement of security services, from headcount-based to outcome-based contracting (OBC). The government has taken the lead on this front and from May 1 2020, all government agencies will be required to adopt security OBCs.

 

Transiting To OBC

Headcount Based contracts

States the required headcount for the assignment and payments is based on number of headcount provided

Task Based Contracts

Specifies requirements that are based on tasks and activities to be completed by Providers. Payment is based on requirements

Outcome Based Contract (OBC)

Spells out outcomes which Buyers expect from Providers. Payment is based on the performance of the Providers in achieving the agreed outcomes
